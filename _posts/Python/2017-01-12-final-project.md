### ğŸ“ Assessment

Assessments are evidence. You're used to seeing them as tests or exit slips, but they're also reflections, rubrics, conversations, code you submit, drawings...whatever shows a teacher you've mastered an idea or a skill.

Any good lesson includes **formative** assessment that occurs _while_ people are learning and allows you to see where they're getting confused and need help (or where they are _destroying_ the work and need to pushed harder (ğŸ‘‹ pretty much each of you at some point this semester).

Teachers also use **summative** assessments at the end of a unit, usually comparing students to a baseline or grade standards.

You will only be teaching for one day (at least for now), so you probably won't be giving a test. **BUT** â€“ you still need to think about assessment.
